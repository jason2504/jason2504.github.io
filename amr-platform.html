AMR Platform Design

Autonomous Mobile Robot Platform for Industrial Applications

Key Achievement

Led 5-engineer team developing 2-ton capacity tracked platform for industrial 6-DOF robotic arm, achieving sub-160-micron precision through comprehensive FEA validation. Delivered complete $90,000 design supporting extreme temperature operation (-40°C to 30°C) with 6-hour runtime for autonomous welding and additive manufacturing applications.

Project Overview

This senior capstone project involved designing a proof-of-concept autonomous mobile robot (AMR) platform to support a Yaskawa GP-50 6-DOF robotic arm for large-scale robotic metal additive manufacturing and heavy-duty industrial welding. The platform needed to be rugged, all-terrain, and capable of operating continuously in extreme environments—both indoors and outdoors.

Design Requirements

Our client specified demanding requirements that pushed the boundaries of conventional mobile robotics design. The platform needed to support a 2-ton robotic payload while maintaining precision sufficient for high-quality welding operations. Additionally, it had to traverse 15% grade inclines, maintain stability during high-precision arm operations, and endure harsh temperature ranges from −40°C to 30°C.

Design Philosophy and Approach

We prioritized robustness and reliability over weight optimization, recognizing that industrial welding and additive manufacturing applications demand unwavering precision and dependability. Our approach centered on comprehensive structural analysis, thorough component selection, and systematic validation of all design decisions through finite element analysis.

Complete AMR platform design: Featuring tracked mobility system, integrated battery enclosure, and mounting interface for 6-DOF robotic arm. The design emphasizes structural rigidity and component accessibility for industrial maintenance.

Mobility System Design

After evaluating wheels, mecanum wheels, and tracks for mobility, we selected a tracked system due to its superior weight distribution on uneven terrain. Tracks provide better performance on loose and packed dirt—the typical working environment for our target applications. We also considered adding spider outriggers to improve stability, but center of mass analysis revealed this complexity was unnecessary for our design envelope.

Power and Control Systems

For propulsion, we evaluated hydraulic motors but selected electric motors for their simplicity and higher energy efficiency. The power system features two 75 HP inverter-duty electric motors powered by 24 LG Lithium-Ion Pacifica modules with 62.4 kWh capacity. This configuration enables 3.5km of maneuvering on tracks while supporting continuous welding operations and robotic arm control.

Structural Engineering and FEA Analysis

We conducted comprehensive finite element analysis to ensure the platform could handle the 2-ton payload with minimal deflection at the robotic arm's end effector. Our structural design maintained functionality of the precision robotic arm by limiting deflection to under 160 microns—critical for maintaining welding quality and additive manufacturing precision.

Environmental Considerations

The design addresses extreme operating conditions including the ability to traverse 15% inclines, withstand -40°C temperature, and operate both indoors and outdoors. Material selection prioritized thermal stability and structural integrity across the full temperature range while ensuring reliable operation of electronic systems and mechanical components.

Technical Implementation

Mechanical Systems

  • Chassis Material: ST 52-3 steel for main structure, Steel A36 for baseplate and hub components
  • Battery Enclosure: Aluminum 6061 for weight reduction while maintaining structural integrity
  • Track System: Bobcat T76 interchangeable tracks for proven durability and serviceability
  • Robotic Interface: Custom baseplate design for Yaskawa GP-50 6-DOF arm mounting
  • Sensor Integration: GNSS compass, 2D LiDAR, stereo cameras, and shaft encoders

Power and Control

  • Motors: Two 75 HP inverter-duty electric motors with redundant control systems
  • Battery System: 24 LG Lithium-Ion Pacifica modules selected for energy density and safety
  • Range of Motion: 180° lateral and 230° vertical robotic arm operation
  • Emergency Systems: Multiple emergency stop systems and fail-safe protocols
  • Navigation: Multi-sensor fusion for precise positioning and autonomous operation

Finite Element Analysis Results

Our comprehensive FEA analysis validated the structural design under worst-case loading conditions. Maximum von Mises stress reached 62 MPa at the chassis base—well below the ST 52-3 steel yield strength of 355 MPa, providing a robust safety margin. Most critically, end-effector deflection remained under 160 microns, ensuring precision robotic operations.

Manufacturing and Cost Analysis

The design required only four custom manufactured parts: chassis, baseplate, battery enclosure, and hub. Manufacturing cost analysis included weld length calculations, shop rates, and setup margins. We validated manufacturing feasibility through industry partner consultations and cost estimation through platforms like Xometry.

Design Trade-offs and Engineering Decisions

Mobility System Selection

We prioritized stability over portability by selecting tracks instead of wheels. While this increased overall weight and complexity, it dramatically improved performance on uneven terrain—essential for outdoor industrial applications. The track system also provided superior load distribution, critical for our 2-ton payload requirement.

Material Selection Strategy

To meet cost requirements, we opted for proven steel alloys instead of exotic lightweight materials. This decision slightly increased weight but ensured cost-efficiency, manufacturing familiarity, and field serviceability. Our material choices balanced performance requirements with practical considerations for industrial deployment.

Power System Architecture

Electric motors were chosen over hydraulic alternatives for their energy efficiency, control precision, and reduced maintenance requirements. The battery system design prioritized energy density and safety, with LG Pacifica modules providing proven performance in automotive applications.

Structural Design Philosophy

Our design philosophy emphasized over-engineering critical load paths while optimizing non-structural components for weight and cost. The chassis design concentrated material where structural analysis indicated highest stress concentrations, ensuring long-term durability under harsh operating conditions.
