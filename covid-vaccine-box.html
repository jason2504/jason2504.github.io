COVID-19 Vaccine Cold Box

Design and Deployment for Global Health Impact

Project Overview

This project involved engineering a critical medical device for COVID-19 vaccine distribution in resource-limited settings. Working with the University of Alberta's Advanced Composite Material Engineering Group, I designed and developed a prototype long-range vaccine cold box capable of maintaining ultra-low temperatures required for vaccine preservation during transport to remote locations in Mozambique.

Design Challenge

The COVID-19 pandemic created unprecedented challenges for vaccine distribution, particularly in developing countries where cold chain infrastructure was limited. The challenge was to design a portable, reliable, and secure cold storage solution that could:

  • Maintain temperatures from -78.5°C to 8°C for extended periods
  • Operate without external power sources in remote locations
  • Prevent unauthorized access while remaining user-friendly
  • Exceed WHO requirements for vaccine transport duration

Technical Innovation

The core innovation was the development of an X-pattern rotary locking mechanism using 2-bar linkages. This mechanism provided security against unauthorized disassembly while maintaining ease of use for healthcare workers. The design balanced mechanical complexity with field reliability.

Performance Achievement

Through comprehensive finite element analysis (FEA) and thermal simulations in SolidWorks, the final design achieved 168-hour cold life performance—significantly exceeding the 96-hour WHO requirement. This represented a 75% improvement over the minimum standard, providing crucial safety margins for real-world deployment scenarios.

Real-World Impact

Field Deployment

The prototype was successfully field-tested in Mozambique in 2023, directly contributing to COVID-19 vaccine distribution infrastructure. This real-world validation demonstrated the design's effectiveness in challenging environmental conditions.

Performance Validation

Field testing confirmed the design's ability to maintain vaccine viability throughout extended transport missions, validating both the thermal performance modeling and mechanical durability under real-world conditions.

  • Field Support: Provided technical support for field testing and deployment in Mozambique
