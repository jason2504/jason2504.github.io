Dual-Mode Robotic Systems

For Manipulation and Locomotion

Key Achievement

Successfully designed and prototyped a novel dual-mode robotic system capable of both precision manipulation (250g payload, ±2mm accuracy) and dynamic locomotion support. Developed two distinct architectural approaches: a rigid joint-based system with universal joint innovation and a continuum cable-actuated design, both featuring a compliant 3-finger gripper with worm gear reduction enabling seamless mode transitions.

Research conducted at University of Pennsylvania's GRASP Laboratory with Dr. Nadia Figueroa, targeting applications in search & rescue, exploration robotics, and adaptive service systems.

Project Overview

This ongoing research project at the University of Pennsylvania's GRASP Laboratory addresses a fundamental challenge in robotics: creating a single system that excels at both precision manipulation and dynamic locomotion. Current robotic systems typically specialize in either mobility (quadrupeds like Boston Dynamics' Spot) or manipulation (industrial arms), but rarely achieve both capabilities cost-effectively within a <$3,000 prototype budget.

Drawing inspiration from biological systems—particularly Dr. Octopus from Marvel comics and natural organisms like primates that use limbs for both locomotion and manipulation—the project explores dual-function limb designs. The research challenges conventional robotics paradigms by creating arms that serve as both precision manipulators and adaptive locomotion supporters.

Design Evolution: Two Architectural Approaches

Version 1: Rigid Joint-Based System

The initial design employed a 4-DOF configuration with custom universal joints at the shoulder, providing 2-DOF motion through timing belt transmission and bevel gear coupling. This approach demonstrated dual-mode feasibility but revealed limitations in terrain adaptability and mechanical complexity requiring precision-machined components.

Version 2: Cable-Actuated Continuum System

The second iteration transitioned to a continuum arm architecture featuring 6 triangular vertebrae connected by TPU springs and controlled via fishing line tendons. This design provides infinite degrees of freedom through 3-motor cable coordination, offering superior terrain adaptation and eliminating complex universal joint mechanisms.

Unified Gripper Innovation

Both iterations feature the same fundamental innovation: a compliant 3-finger gripper with 60:1 worm gear reduction, spring-loaded compliance, and self-locking capability. This enables seamless transitions between delicate object manipulation (250g payload) and robust ground contact support during locomotion.

Performance Targets and Validation

Comprehensive torque analysis using SolidWorks mass properties validates servo adequacy across operational modes, achieving manipulation precision of ±2mm with 5-50N force range, 1-2m/s locomotion speed capability, and <1s mode switching time within <10kg total system weight.

System Architecture and Component Selection

Core Electronics Package

The system employs a sophisticated electronics architecture designed for autonomous operation:

Computational and Sensing
  • NVIDIA Jetson Orin NX 16GB (10-25W) - Central processing for real-time control and AI
  • Adafruit BNO085 IMU - 9-DOF sensor fusion at 1000Hz for balance
  • Dual Arducam Fisheye Cameras - 160° field of view for environmental awareness
Power and Actuation
  • 4x Samsung 21700 batteries (25A continuous, 5000mAh each) - Steady-state power
  • TATTU R-Line 14.8V LiPo (240A burst) - Dynamic servo movements
  • U2D2 Power Hub - USB to servo communication with 20A distribution

Actuation Systems Comparison

Version 1: Rigid Joint System
  • 5x STS3215 Smart Servos per arm (6.5 kg·cm continuous torque)
  • Universal joint with timing belt transmission and bevel gear coupling
  • MXL timing belts and custom 3D-printed precision components
  • Total system mass: 3.17kg (2 arms) or 4.67kg (4 arms)
Version 2: Continuum System
  • 3x Dynamixel XM430-W210-T per arm (310.39 N·cm stall torque)
  • 6 triangular vertebrae with TPU inter-vertebral springs
  • 0.3mm monofilament fishing line with distributed cable routing
  • Infinite DOF through cooperative cable coordination

Manufacturing Strategy

Additive manufacturing approach enables rapid prototyping and cost-effective production:

  • Bambu Lab PLA Basic (adjusted: 1464 MPa modulus, 0.66 g/cm³ density with 15% infill)
  • Optimized print orientation with Z-axis aligned to arm length for maximum strength
  • Hybrid approach combining 3D printed structures with off-the-shelf precision elements

Technical Analysis and Performance Validation

Torque Analysis and Servo Sizing Verification

Manipulation Mode
  • Maximum payload: 250g with 5× acceleration and 2× safety factors
  • Total force required: 24.5N across 3 gripper fingers
  • Required torque: 1.96 N·m with available 2.58 N·m (safety margin)
Locomotion Mode
  • Rolling resistance: 0.06 coefficient on smooth surfaces
  • Acceleration target: 0.5 m/s² with 2.0 safety factor
  • All servos provide sufficient torque for rolling locomotion

Design Evolution Rationale

Version 2 addresses key limitations identified in Version 1 analysis:

  • Terrain Adaptation: Continuum design provides superior surface conformance vs. discrete joints
  • Mechanical Simplification: Cable actuation eliminates complex universal joint precision requirements
  • Workspace Enhancement: Infinite DOF enables unlimited positioning within operational envelope
  • Impact Resistance: Inherent compliance provides safety and damage tolerance

Control Development Strategy

Hardware-centric approach acknowledging continuum mechanics modeling limitations:

  • Genesis Physics Engine with GPU acceleration for parallel environment training
  • Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) for stable continuous control during mode transitions
  • Real-world reinforcement learning prioritizing empirical adaptation over theoretical models
  • URDF conversion from SolidWorks preserving mass properties and kinematic relationships
