Hi, I'm Jason Chen. I am pursuing my Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering and Applied Mechanics with a concentration in Mechatronics and Robotic Systems at the University of Pennsylvania. Currently, I'm a Graduate Research Assistant at the GRASP Laboratory in the Figueroa Robotics Lab, working under Dr. Nadia Figueroa. My research interests focus on the intersection of mechanical design, robotics, and automation. I am passionate about creating innovative robotic systems that seamlessly integrate mechanical design with intelligent control for real-world applications.

I earned my Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering Co-op from the University of Alberta, where I gained extensive hands-on experience through internships and research projects. My work has ranged from designing COVID-19 vaccine transport systems deployed in Mozambique to developing autonomous mobile robot platforms for industrial applications. I have experience in advanced manufacturing, FEA simulation, project management, and developing automated workflow solutions that have improved efficiency across multiple engineering teams.

As a Certified SOLIDWORKS Professional (CSWP) and with a background spanning composite materials research, precision mechanical design, and robotics system integration, I bring a multidisciplinary approach to solving complex engineering challenges. I'm particularly excited about projects that bridge the gap between innovative design and practical implementation.
