Dual-Mode Robotic Systems for Manipulation and Locomotion

University of Pennsylvania, GRASP Laboratory, Figueroa Robotics Lab (April 2025 - Present)
Currently designing and prototyping innovative dual-mode robotic systems with 4-DOF per arm capable of both precision manipulation and dynamic locomotion. Developing two distinct approaches: a rigid joint-based system with custom universal joints and servo mechanisms, and a cable-actuated continuum arm with distributed motor control and flexible vertebrae. Engineering novel actuation systems including timing belt transmission with bevel gear coupling and cooperative cable control for enhanced system redundancy. Designed compliant 3-finger gripper with worm gear reduction and spring-based adaptive control for seamless transitions between delicate manipulation and robust ground contact support.

Tubular Braided Composites with Near-Zero Thermal Expansion

University of Alberta Dean Research Award (Sep 2023 - Apr 2024)
Characterized compressive properties of epoxy resin and investigated failure modes under various loading conditions using digital image correlation (DIC) analysis. Manufactured tubular braided composites with Kevlar prepregs using maypole braiding machine, systematically optimizing braid angles (35°-55°) and epoxy-hardener ratios. Successfully achieved near-zero coefficient of thermal expansion composites for aerospace applications through iterative design optimization. Utilized DaVis software and 2D stereo camera systems for precise strain analysis and thermal deformation tracking. Validated epoxy isotropy and homogeneity, demonstrating different failure modes under tension (brittle) versus compression (ductile). View the research poster here.

COVID-19 Vaccine Cold Box Design and Deployment

University of Alberta, Advanced Composite Material Engineering Group (Jan 2022 – Sep 2022)
Engineered a prototype long-range vaccine cold box achieving -78.5°C to 8°C temperature maintenance for COVID-19 vaccine transportation in Mozambique. Designed innovative X-pattern rotary locking mechanism with 2-bar linkages to prevent unauthorized disassembly while maintaining user-friendly operation. Performed comprehensive finite element analysis (FEA) and thermal simulations in SolidWorks, achieving 168-hour cold life performance—significantly exceeding the 96-hour WHO requirement. Selected optimal materials including polyurethane with glass bead reinforcement for housing and glass-filled nylon for mechanical components. Successfully field-tested prototype in Mozambique in 2023, directly contributing to COVID-19 vaccine distribution infrastructure.
